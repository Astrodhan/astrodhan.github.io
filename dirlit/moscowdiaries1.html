I looked at my basket full of groceries and thought “Oh wow now I am fully an adult.”.

I took a picture and sent it to Lisa, a 22 year old moscowite. It was my first week in Moscow, and a stranger from the internet was my only friend here.

Lisa was severly disappointed that I was taking pride in doing something so basic. As I would slowly discover, 16 years olds lived completely independently here. A 22 year old grown ass adult wowing at a basket of groceries was a major turn-off.

What was I supposed to tell her? That I had never cooked full-time for myself until now? I told her to never mind instead and slipped out of that topic like an oiled rabbit through a brass ring.


Or could I say, “Oh I am just from a different culture”. Moscow 2021 was a strange post-feminist Russia, while the India I had just come from was pre-feminist.


I took my groceries, and headed for my hostel.
